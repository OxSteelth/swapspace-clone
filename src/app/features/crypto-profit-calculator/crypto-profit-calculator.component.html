FAQ

How are crypto gains calculated?

Crypto profits (or losses) are calculated by subtracting the cost of investment (including your initial investment amount plus any fees) from the current value of investment. If by the time you sell your chosen crypto goes up in price enough to offset the fees, your crypto investment becomes profitable.

Why invest in crypto?

Cryptocurrencies are a popular investment for a number of reasons.
  • While high volatility can lead to some unexpected losses, it can bring large gains as well. For example, people who figured out how to invest in Bitcoin early on enjoyed substantial profits.
  • Many people invest in cryptocurrencies to diversify their portfolios and hedge against downturns in the traditional markets.
  • There’s a wide variety of projects available for investing, so almost everyone who sees the potential in technology and innovation it brings can pick something suited to their interest (moreover, new crypto to invest in appears on the market pretty regularly).
  • Crypto is decentralized, which attracts investors who appreciate financial independence and relative anonymity, as well as freedom from censorship and safety from regulatory meddling.
  • Crypto markets are open 24/7 which is appealing to many investors, as this allows more flexibility with their investments and easier access to their accounts.

Tips for the beginner crypto investor

There are some important pieces of advice to remember if you’re interested in investing in cryptocurrency.
  • Learn the trade: as an investor, you will benefit from understanding both fundamentals and technicals. Try to understand what and why is happening with the markets.
  • Start small: since the cryptocurrency market is inherently volatile, don’t invest more than you are ready to lose.
  • Diversify your crypto portfolio: spreading your funds across different cryptocurrencies instead of putting the whole investment into one crypto project helps mitigating risks and offset potential losses.
  • Keep tabs on the market: regularly check trends, news, etc., so if the conditions on the crypto market change, you can detect this early and be ready to act
  • Secure your investment: due to cryptocurrencies’ decentralized nature, there’s a higher level of responsibility when it comes to keeping your funds safe. Use only reputable exchanges, secure wallets, and beware the crypto scams.
  • Have a defined investment strategy: you should be clear on your goals, acceptable risks, tools, how long you’ll hold your crypto, etc.
  • Keep a level head: the volatility of crypto prices can lead to panic or impulsive decisions. Be patient and remember your goals.
  • Brush up on regulations: many countries already have some rules and laws concerning crypto investments, such as tax regulations. Find that information out early, so that you don’t get in the hot water with the law later.
  • Do your own research: don’t forget to find out everything you can about your potential investment.
